Infrastructure engineers are accustomed to thinking in failure modes, operational tolerances, handoffs, and partial degradation. Many AI products are still built as if those concerns arrive later.

The most durable AI systems are usually the ones designed with infrastructure instincts from the start:

- clear failure boundaries,
- observable workflows,
- constrained autonomy,
- human override,
- and explicit tradeoffs between latency, cost, and trust.

That mindset is especially useful when AI touches operational environments. The question is not whether a model can produce a compelling answer. The question is whether the full system remains understandable when the answer is incomplete, late, ambiguous, or wrong.
