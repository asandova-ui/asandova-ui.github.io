When a tool is meant for engineers, analysts, or network operators, interface decisions matter as much as model quality. Good operator-facing software reduces ambiguity, makes provenance visible, and respects the pace of real decisions.

Some useful questions:

- What should be shown first when attention is scarce?
- Which output needs a source trace?
- Where does the system need stronger defaults instead of more freedom?
- What is the fallback state when the confidence is weak?

These are product questions, but they are also engineering questions. In high-trust environments, usability and system credibility are tightly linked.
